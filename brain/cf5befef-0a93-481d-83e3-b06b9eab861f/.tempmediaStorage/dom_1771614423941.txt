	1. INTRODUCTION
	1.1 The Ayurvedic Concept of Beauty (Saundarya)
	In Ayurveda, beauty is not merely a superficial quality but a reflection of inner health, balance, and vitality. The Sanskrit term for beauty,
	Saundarya
	, encompasses a holistic state of well-being, including physical attractiveness, mental clarity, and spiritual essence. A truly beautiful person, according to the classics, possesses a combination of healthy, glowing skin (Prasanna), a pleasant demeanor, and a long, healthy life .
	This module delves into the practical application of Ayurvedic wisdom for beauty treatments. It bridges classical therapies described in texts like the
	Sushruta Samhita
	and
	Ashtanga Hridayam
	with modern clinical protocols. You will learn to move beyond generic facials to provide personalized, constitution-based treatments that nourish the skin, hair, and senses, addressing the root cause of imbalances rather than just masking symptoms .
	1.2 Learning Objectives
	Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:
	Differentiate
	between cosmetic and therapeutic beauty treatments based on Ayurvedic principles.
	Assess
	a client's skin and hair type according to the Tridosha theory (Vata, Pitta, Kapha) .
	Demonstrate
	step-by-step protocols for classical Ayurvedic facial and body treatments like Mukhalepa, Abhyanga, and Udvartana .
	Formulate
	basic herbal preparations such as Ubtans, Lepas, and medicated oils for specific beauty concerns .
	Identify
	key Marma points on the face and head and apply appropriate massage techniques for lifting, detoxification, and rejuvenation .
